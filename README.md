**E-Commerce Sales Analytics**

SQL + Tableau Data Analysis Project

Overview

This project analyzes an e-commerce transactions dataset to understand sales performance, product demand, and customer purchasing patterns.

The goal was to move from raw transactional data to actionable business insights using SQL for analysis and Tableau for visualization.

The final outcome is an interactive dashboard that highlights key metrics and helps explore revenue trends across time, product categories, and geographic regions.

**Dataset**

The analysis uses the Olist Brazilian E-Commerce Dataset, which contains detailed information about orders, customers, and products.

Approximate dataset scale:

~100,000 orders

~300,000 order items

Multiple product categories

Customers across several Brazilian states

Dataset source:
https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/olistbr/brazilian-ecommerce

**Key Questions Explored**

This project focuses on answering several practical business questions:

• How has revenue evolved over time?
• Which product categories generate the most revenue?
• Which geographic regions drive the highest sales?
• What is the typical value of a customer order?
• Are most customers returning or one-time buyers?

**Insights**
Revenue Trend

Sales increased steadily during 2017 with the highest revenue observed toward the end of the year, suggesting seasonal purchasing activity.

Product Performance

The Beauty & Health category generated the highest revenue among all product groups.

Regional Distribution

The São Paulo (SP) region contributed the largest share of revenue, reflecting the concentration of customers in this market.

Customer Behavior

Repeat purchase rate is relatively low (~3%), indicating that most purchases come from new customers rather than returning buyers.

Revenue Concentration

A small number of product categories generate a majority of the revenue, following a Pareto-type distribution.
